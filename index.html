Effects of cumulative workload on player injuries (capstone summary)

Student ID 207677808 · Hebrew University

Problem

Estimate the causal effect of short-term workload buildup on overuse injury risk in NBA players.

Data scope

Injury logs + advanced game/player tables, merged into injury opportunities with rolling 7-day load features.

Methods and tools

Propensity score matching, DiD, ITT with exogenous schedule density, falsification on contact injuries, and double ITT carryover analysis.

Key numeric result

Dense schedule exposure increases overuse injury risk by about 25% per game in the treated windows.

  • Naive negative association flips after bias-aware design.
  • Signal persists under robustness checks and injury-type tests.

NBA return-to-play: internal load injuries and team-level medical outcomes

5,000+ documented return events · 15 seasons (2011-2025) · sensitive load injuries (knee, plantar or calf)

Problem

Identify whether some teams return players fast without paying a recurrence penalty in internal load injuries.

Data scope

More than 5,000 documented return events across 2011-2025, focused on knee, plantar, and calf injury types.

Methods and tools

Team-level and within-player comparisons, severity and usage controls, clustered recurrence-gap analysis, and broad-outcome sensitivity checks.

Key numeric result

Fast-return clusters show about 18pp recurrence spread (38% vs 20%, p < 0.001), with about 17pp preserved within-player.

  • Gap persists after severity/timing controls and usage matching.
  • Expanding to all load injuries reduces gap to 8.5pp but keeps it significant.
